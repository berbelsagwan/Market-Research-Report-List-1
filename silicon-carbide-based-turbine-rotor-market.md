<p><p>The Silicon Carbide-based turbine rotor market features two primary types: C/SiC and SiC/SiC. C/SiC (carbon/silicon carbide) combines a carbon fiber matrix with silicon carbide, offering excellent high-temperature resistance and mechanical properties. It is well-suited for aerospace applications. SiC/SiC, on the other hand, utilizes silicon carbide fibers embedded in a silicon carbide matrix, providing superior thermal stability and strength, making it ideal for demanding environments such as power generation and advanced aerospace systems. Both types enhance rotor performance and efficiency.</p></p>
