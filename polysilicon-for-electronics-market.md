<p><p>Polysilicon for electronics is categorized into three distinct grades based on purity and application. Grade I polysilicon, with the highest purity, is primarily used in semiconductor manufacturing for integrated circuits. Grade II polysilicon, slightly less pure, is suitable for certain solar cell applications and less critical electronic components. Grade III polysilicon has the lowest purity and is generally used for less demanding applications, often in the production of lower-end solar panels or as a precursor in alternative materials technologies.</p></p>
