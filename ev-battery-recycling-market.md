<p><p>The EV battery recycling market encompasses various battery types: Nickel-cadmium batteries are usually recycled due to hazardous cadmium content, while nickel-metal hydride batteries are often used in hybrid vehicles and are recyclable for their nickel and rare earth metals. Lithium-ion batteries, commonly found in electric vehicles, are extensively recycled for valuable lithium, cobalt, and other materials. Lithium polymer batteries share similar properties with lithium-ion but are typically lighter. Lead-acid batteries, though older technology, are still widely recycled for lead and sulfuric acid recovery.</p></p>
