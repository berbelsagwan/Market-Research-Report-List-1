<p><p>Controlled Atmosphere Packaging (CAP) is an innovative packaging technology designed to enhance the shelf life and quality of perishable products. This method involves altering the composition of gases within the package, typically by reducing oxygen levels and increasing carbon dioxide levels, which slows down the respiration rate of fruits, vegetables, and other food items. The CAP market is experiencing significant growth due to the rising demand for fresh and minimally processed food products, increased global trade, and a growing focus on food safety and waste reduction.</p><p>In recent years, the market has been influenced by several trends, including advancements in packaging materials that improve barrier properties, as well as the integration of smart packaging technologies that monitor and control the internal atmosphere. Additionally, sustainability is becoming a key consideration, prompting manufacturers to explore eco-friendly materials.
