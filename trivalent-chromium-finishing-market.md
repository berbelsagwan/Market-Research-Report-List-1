<p><p>The trivalent chromium finishing market encompasses three main types: plating, conversion coatings, and passivation. Plating involves depositing a chromium layer onto surfaces for corrosion resistance and aesthetic appeal. Conversion coatings create a protective film through a chemical reaction, enhancing substrate durability and paint adhesion. Passivation refers to the treatment of metal surfaces to form a protective oxide layer, improving corrosion resistance. Together, these methods offer eco-friendly alternatives to hexavalent chromium finishes, supporting various industries seeking sustainable solutions.</p></p>
