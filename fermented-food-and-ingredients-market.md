<p><p>Fermented food and ingredients are products created through the fermentation process, which involves the transformation of raw ingredients by microorganisms such as bacteria, yeast, and molds. This process not only enhances the flavors and textures of the foods but also improves their nutritional value and shelf life. Common examples include yogurt, kimchi, sauerkraut, kombucha, and fermented soy products like miso and tempeh.</p><p>The Fermented Food and Ingredients Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by rising consumer awareness of the health benefits associated with these products, such as improved gut health and enhanced digestion. Additionally, the increasing trend towards natural and organic food options is propelling market expansion, as fermentation is often viewed as a natural preservation method. The demand for probiotics has further contributed to the market's growth, with consumers seeking functional foods that support overall wellness.</p><p>The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.70% during the forecast period. Key trends include innovation in product offerings, the introduction of plant-based fermented foods, and a growing interest in global cuisines that utilize fermentation techniques. These factors combine to position the fermented food and ingredients sector as a dynamic and evolving market.</p></p>
