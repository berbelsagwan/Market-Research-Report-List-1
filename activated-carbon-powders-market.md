<p><ul><li>Physical Method</li><li>Chemical Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The activated carbon powders market can be categorized based on the methods used for activation: physical and chemical. Physical activation involves heating carbonaceous materials in the presence of gases like steam or carbon dioxide, enhancing their porosity. In contrast, chemical activation uses chemical agents, such as phosphoric acid or potassium hydroxide, to create a highly porous structure at lower temperatures. Both methods produce activated carbon powders with distinct characteristics, influencing their applications in water treatment, air purification, and other filtration processes.</p></p>
