<p><p>Simultaneous interpretation equipment is essential for multilingual events, offering two main market types: cable and wireless systems. Cable systems rely on physical connections, providing stable audio quality but limiting mobility. They are often used in controlled environments. In contrast, wireless systems offer greater flexibility and convenience, allowing interpreters to move freely while transmitting audio to listeners via radio frequencies or infrared signals. Both types cater to diverse event needs, balancing connection reliability and user mobility for optimal communication.</p></p>
