<p><ul><li>ACH Method</li><li>Isobutylene Method</li><li>Ethylene Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Methyl Methacrylate (MMA) is primarily produced through three methods: the ACH process, Isobutylene method, and Ethylene method. The ACH method utilizes acetone and hydrogen cyanide, offering high purity but involves significant safety concerns. The Isobutylene method employs isobutylene and methacrylic acid, providing an efficient way to produce MMA with competitive costs. Lastly, the Ethylene method utilizes ethylene-derived feedstocks, focusing on sustainability and lower environmental impact. Each method varies in production efficiency, safety, and environmental considerations.</p></p>
