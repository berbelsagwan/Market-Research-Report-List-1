<p><p>The food waste disposer market primarily comprises two types: pulverizing and drying. Pulverizing types break down food scraps into smaller pieces, facilitating easy disposal through plumbing systems, making them ideal for households and restaurants seeking convenience. Conversely, drying types reduce food waste by evaporating moisture, transforming scraps into a dry, lightweight material for composting or incineration. Both types cater to sustainability goals, with pulverizing focusing on immediate waste management and drying enhancing long-term waste reduction strategies.</p></p>
